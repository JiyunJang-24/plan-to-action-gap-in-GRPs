Planning-to-Action Gap in Robot Foundation Models

Qualitative examples: reasonable visual plans, unreliable action execution
Jiyun Jang · Korea University

Overview

Recent robot foundation models can often generate semantically reasonable future trajectories or videos in new environments. However, these plans do not always translate into reliable executable robot actions. This page shows simple qualitative examples of this planning-to-action gap.

Interpretation

These examples motivate studying whether current robot policies sufficiently understand the relationship between visual observations, robot action coordinate systems, and embodiment-dependent action effects. Reducing this gap may be important for robust zero-shot manipulation.
